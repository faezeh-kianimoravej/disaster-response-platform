# Risks

**Risk 01:**

Not understanding the real client need due to domain terminology translations.

Mitigations:
- Take notes of all domain terminology, descibe them in your own interpretation. Have your client validate if you understanding of it is correct. Take extra note of words that are used in multiple domains (e.g. develop, repository) and validate that your shared understandig of said term is the same.
- Walk through a hypothetical scenario with your client, give concrete results for every step. Validate with your client as you go through the scenario.

**Risk 02:**

Delivery not matching the expectation of the client.

Mitigations:
- Expectation management: Communicate about specific user stories that will be delivered during this sprint as well as long term expectations for releases.
- Continuous communication: Schedule returning, standardized meetings for periodic updates. Keep the client involved/ updated.
- Be transparant: Don't hide encountered hurdles which affect delays of delivery. Communicate and find a solution together or reset expectations.


**Risk 03:**

Only one person understands a key part of the product, this person is unavailabe for a long time/ permanently, seriously impacting the development of product. 

Mitigations:
- Don't work in isolation, involve at least one other person from each related domain in the design phase and update them during the implementation about progress.
- Require code/ design reviews from all related teammember involved. 
- Provide solid documentation from gettting started to architectural design.
- Keep code clean and self explanatory.
