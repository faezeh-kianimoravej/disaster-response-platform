## Minutes

### Discussion Summary
- The team discussed the feedback received from the client and analyzed what went well and what could be improved.  
- It was clarified that the team **understood the client’s expectations correctly**, but **the sprint planning and time management were not optimal** in the previous sprint.  

### Key Comments from the Retro
1. *“We could improve our daily communication by sharing updates about our tasks more actively throughout the day, not just during the daily meeting.”*  
2. *“Standup meetings sometimes take too long.”*  
3. *“Not understanding client demand properly — an end-to-end integration must have been done in this sprint which was not accomplished.”*  

---

## Notes
- The team collectively agreed on the need to **align sprint planning with the real capacity** of each member to avoid overcommitment and improve predictability.  
- Everyone acknowledged that **daily communication and visibility of progress** have already started to improve, and the team is **actively working on maintaining that consistency day by day**.  

---

## Actions and Decisions

1. **Define team capacity during sprint planning** — assign tasks based on each member’s available time.  
2. **Timebox meetings strictly:**  
   - Daily Standup: **10–15 minutes**  
   - Sprint Planning: approximately **1 hour per week of sprint** (e.g., 2-hour session for a 2-week sprint).  
3. **Focus on delivering smaller but complete features**, ensuring functionality is end-to-end and demo-ready.  
4. **Retro board** must be **completed before the coaching session**, and team members should vote on the most important topics in advance.  
5. Continue improving **team communication** outside of daily meetings through active task updates.
