## Minutes

The team discussed the Q1 grade and shared their overall feelings about the result. While the grade was acknowledged, the team emphasized the importance of understanding the reasoning behind the assessment in order to improve in Q2.

It was mentioned that the first peer review was not very helpful. However, the feedback received after Q1 was considered more useful. The team felt that receiving this feedback earlier during Q1 would have supported better and faster improvements.

The team also discussed that Ivo mainly provides feedback from a client perspective. While this perspective is valuable, the team expressed that they sometimes need feedback from a teacher’s perspective to better understand academic expectations and grading criteria.

Regarding Q2, the team acknowledged that there should be more focus on testing and redesigning services. At the same time, it was noted that Q2 has a different rubric, which introduces additional focus points that need attention beyond testing and redesign.

The importance of having a clear and concrete understanding of the MVP for Q2 was discussed. The team agreed that this should be aligned with Ivo to ensure clarity on what needs to be implemented.

The team reflected that it would have been beneficial to discuss and explain their service design decisions with Dick before the realization of Q1. As a suggestion for improvement, Deepak recommended documenting all major technical decisions in a shared document so that all stakeholders can review and understand them.

Finally, the team expressed the need to clearly understand the basic requirements for achieving a passing grade. Sepideh will raise these concerns and related issues in the student committee to seek further clarification.
